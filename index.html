Data Analysis of Student State Achievement Test Scores

I volunteered to participate in the East Tennessee Science Partnership Grant between Blount County Schools and Maryville College while a special education teacher in Blount County. As part of the grant, I was trained how to read and interpret the state achievement student test scores. I completed a detailed data analysis of each my students' test data. I was able to chart specific missed skills and matched them to their related state subject matter goals/standards for each student. Next, I created charts of each subject matter goal and listed the students needing retaught that goal. I spent the next school year teaching the normal curriculum along with reteaching missed items from the previous year's state test. In one school year, majority of my special education students improved from non-proficient in most subjects to proficient in two to three subjects. I have included a few screen views of some of my charts and data with identifying information removed. Under Student C or J, c stands for correct answer and w stands for wrong answer.
